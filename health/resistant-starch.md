# Resistant starch

- **low-carb / keto-friendly**: doesn't break down into glucose in the small
  intestine
- **prebiotic**: feeds gut bacteria
- **typical supplement range**: start with 1 tbsp and increase to 2 in a week
- **gelatinizes at ~60Â°C** and becomes digestible

## Limited scientific evidence benefits

- reduces appetite
- improves inflammatory biomarkers
- improves fasting glucose and insulin
- improves insulin resistance and sensitivity
- reduces the risk of type 2 diabetes
- reduces low-density cholesterol

## Potato starch

Use with:

- cold water
- yogurt
- cooled food

### Home extraction

- peel potatoes
- grate finely
- initial extraction:
  - cover with room-temp water
  - massage with hands a few minutes
  - strain / squeeze the water with released starch
  - optionally repeat
- settling:
  - let the water sit for 30-60 min
  - pour off the dirty brown water without disturbing the sludge at the bottom
  - add clean cold water to the white sludge, stir it up until dissolved
  - repeat 2-3 times
- dry for 1-2 days
- optionally grind, but avoid heating in the process

### Bought potato starch test

- squeak test: rub it between fingers - it should feel crunchy and make a faint
  squeaking sound
- water test: mix into cold water - should not dissolve, but settle in 20 min
  with clean water on top
